Stanford researcher's 4-D Camera's for Underground Mining

(For picture, click on anywhere in the image to change the focus, you can also pan around the image)

During my trip to the bay area I caught up with DD, one of my good friends from my PhD at the Australian Centre for Field Robotics, Sydney.

Don's research area is an advanced type of camera called a light-field.

"This one camera is like a whole bunch of little cameras all getting a different view of the scene."

Don thinks this will be critical for the next generation of mining sensors.

"light fields could let us get a high quality image of a rock face in low light before it's blasted"

"light field camera's have no moving parts but can sense depth. They could be more reliable than the lasers used on autonomous vehicles"
